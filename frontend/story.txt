I started running in November.

I had no goal, no race, no dream, and no plan.

I simply wanted to run.

At the end of November I started going to the gym.

There I met a senior runner named Srivatsav.

One day I asked if I could join one of his runs.

My first run was a 5K.

Soon after I completed my first 10K with a group of runners.

December became the month that built momentum.

During the last three weeks of December I completed more than 70 km.

Then Srivatsav left for an internship.

Even after he left, I continued following the path he inspired me to start.

I set my first serious goal:
Participate in the Intramural 5K race in March.

January and February passed while I trained consistently.

March arrived.

I participated in the 5K race.

I lost.

I blamed myself.

I questioned my preparation and effort.

But I continued showing up.

Soon after, I participated in a 1.5 km race.

I finished 4th place.

The final 300 meters were the hardest and fastest effort I had ever produced.

During that final sprint, something changed mentally.

For the first time, I felt pure excitement while competing.

For the first time, I realized:
"This is what I want."

This was the moment when running stopped being just an activity and started becoming part of my identity.

This event represents the transition from casually running to genuinely wanting to pursue running seriously.

The website should treat this moment as one of the emotional peaks of the entire story.

On March 20, another senior runner named Samuel invited me to join a Half Marathon.

Until then I had never crossed 10 km.

Samuel shared many lessons and experiences with me.

During one conversation he told me:
"You are like me."

That sentence stayed with me.

I completed my first Half Marathon in approximately 2 hours and 6 minutes.

Near the end Samuel suffered cramps.

During the final kilometers I helped him continue.

We crossed the finish together.

That became one of the most memorable moments of my running journey.

Running was not my only challenge.

My journey also included:
- Pull-ups
- Push-ups
- Weight gain struggles
- Pole climbing
- Physical discipline
- Mental resilience

On April 25, 2026, I completed the TCS World 10K Bengaluru Virtual Run.

Later my medal arrived.

After completing the run, I saw a reel by Zoya Ahmed about the Bengaluru event.

I commented about completing the virtual edition and receiving my medal.

The comment received more than 260 likes.

It was a small moment, but it made me feel connected to a much larger running community.

Recently I crossed 500 km on Strava despite training in the intense summer heat of Andhra Pradesh.

One day I watched a reel about the Procam Slam.

That became my dream.

After B.Tech I want to travel across India and complete:

- TCS World 10K Bengaluru
- Tata Mumbai Marathon
- Vedanta Delhi Half Marathon
- TCS World 25K Kolkata

and earn the Procam Slam.

Target year:
2028 or 2029.
